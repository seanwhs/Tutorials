# Appendix A33 — The Engineering Reading Canon

## The Books That Changed Software Engineering

> **Purpose:** Software engineering is one of the few professions where most practitioners never study the foundational literature of their field. This appendix provides a curated reading canon for engineers who want to develop not merely technical skills, but engineering judgment.

---

# Introduction

The biggest misconception about books is:

```text id="read001"
Books teach
technology.
```

The best books teach:

```text id="read002"
How to think.
```

---

Technologies change:

```text id="read003"
Every year.
```

Principles survive:

```text id="read004"
For decades.
```

---

# Final Thought

The difference between junior engineers and principal engineers is rarely intelligence.

It is usually:

```text id="read055"
The number
of great ideas

they have
borrowed
from others.
```

Because software engineering is one of the few professions where you can inherit decades of experience simply by reading.
