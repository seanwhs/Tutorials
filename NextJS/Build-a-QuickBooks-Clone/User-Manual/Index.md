# Comprehensive User Guide: The Complete Bookkeeping Cycle — Master Index

A 7-part guide walking through one full accounting cycle — from opening a brand new company's books, through a realistic month of transactions, to closing the month and producing trustworthy financial statements — using a single fictitious small business (**Riverside Design Studio**, a graphic design consultancy owned by **Maya Chen**) as a running example throughout every part.

This guide complements the conceptual Appendices B through H (which explain individual accounting concepts in isolation) by showing everything working together, in the order a real bookkeeper actually experiences it across a month. It also complements the code-focused tutorial series (Parts 0-24) and Appendix A by showing the same underlying discipline (double-entry, atomic transactions, reconciliation, reporting) applied to a concrete, narrative business scenario rather than abstract code or isolated worked examples.

---

## The 7 Parts

**Part 1: Meet the Company and Opening the Books**
Introduces Riverside Design Studio, builds its Chart of Accounts (customized for a service business — no inventory/COGS), records the owner's opening cash and equipment investment, and confirms the opening Balance Sheet balances. Sets up initial customers (Bloom & Co, Northgate Dental, Petal & Pine Events) and vendors (Adobe, WeWork, Jordan Reyes).

**Part 2: Weeks 1-2 — Sales, Invoicing, and Customer Payments**
Walks through a cash sale, two credit-sale invoices (Bloom & Co $1,200, Northgate Dental $4,500), the first automatically-generated recurring retainer invoice (Petal & Pine $600), a full invoice payment, and an early-payment-discount payment. Ends with a mid-month sanity-check tally.

**Part 3: Weeks 3-4 — Bills, Contractor Expense, and Owner Transactions**
Walks through two vendor bills arriving (Adobe, WeWork), paying one off, subcontracting illustration work to a freelancer (with a note on contractor vs. employee treatment), a business credit card purchase, the first depreciation entry on the owner-contributed laptop, and an owner's draw. Ends with a full running-balance summary across every account.

**Part 4: Bank Reconciliation and Month-End Adjusting Entries**
Walks through pulling the real bank statement, finding a $12 discrepancy, tracing it to an unrecorded bank fee, recording it, and confirming the reconciliation is complete. Reviews AR/AP aging before closing and confirms no other accrued items are missing. Clarifies the difference between routine monthly closing and formal annual books-closing.

**Part 5: Trial Balance and Financial Statements**
Builds the full Trial Balance (confirming total debits = total credits across the whole month), then the Profit & Loss report, the Balance Sheet (with a live proof that Assets = Liabilities + Equity, fully reconciled to $21,002), and both AR and AP Aging reports in their final month-end form — all built purely from reorganizing the same underlying journal entries from Parts 2-4.

**Part 6: Interpreting Results, Closing the Month, and Starting the Next Cycle**
Walks through actually reading the P&L and Balance Sheet like a business owner (margins, ratios, revenue mix, liquidity), clarifies that no formal year-end closing is needed monthly, covers the business decisions Maya makes based on January's numbers, and shows how February's Balance Sheet carries forward from January's ending balances while Income/Expense accounts keep accumulating for the fiscal year.

**Part 7: Quick Reference Checklist and Full Cycle Summary**
Distills Parts 1-6 into a reusable 30-step checklist across 6 phases (Opening, Day-to-Day Recording, Month-End Close, Reading Reports, Repeat, Formal Year-End Closing), plus quick-reference tables for account normal balances and common transaction patterns. Designed to be kept open as a working reference once the underlying concepts are understood.

## How This Guide Relates to the Rest of the Knowledge Base

- **Appendices B-H**: teach individual accounting concepts in isolation (double-entry theory, Chart of Accounts structure, 34 worked transaction examples, reading financial statements, glossary, common mistakes, accrual vs cash). This guide shows those same concepts applied together, in sequence, on one continuous business scenario.
- **Tutorial series (Parts 0-24) and Appendix A**: teach how to BUILD the software application that automates this cycle. This guide teaches the underlying accounting cycle itself, independent of any specific software — the same cycle applies whether using the tutorial's QuickBooks clone, real QuickBooks, or a fully manual paper ledger.
