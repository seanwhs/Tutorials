## 1. The FinOps Philosophy

You have moved beyond the developer's traditional focus on "getting it to work" to the owner's focus on "keeping it profitable." By formalizing a **FinOps approach**—where engineering, finance, and business objectives converge—you have established a framework to manage costs as a first-class feature of the system.

## 2. Key Economic Pillars

* **The "Pay-Per-Usage" Model:** By leveraging serverless architecture for compute and API-driven services (Inngest, Sanity, Clerk) for backend logic, you ensure that infrastructure costs scale linearly with your success, not with idle time.
* **Cost-Aware Workflows:** You have designed the system to distinguish between the **Critical Path** (Check-in/Auth) and **Background Tasks** (Emails/Analytics). This allows you to optimize costs for high-volume, low-priority tasks without sacrificing user experience.
* **Pricing Strategy:** By aligning your tiered pricing (Starter/Business/Enterprise) with your technical cost drivers (Events/Attendees/Storage), you ensure that your revenue model remains inherently profitable.

## 3. The "Maturity Journey"

You have defined the natural evolution of your platform, providing a clear path for growth:

1. **Prototype:** Validating the technical concept.
2. **Production System:** Ensuring reliability and operational excellence.
3. **Enterprise Platform:** Introducing compliance, governance, and auditability.
4. **Commercial SaaS:** Optimizing for unit economics, scalability, and market-ready pricing.
