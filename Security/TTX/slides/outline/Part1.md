# Slide 4 – What Is a Tabletop Exercise?

### Definition

A facilitated discussion-based exercise where participants walk through their responses to a simulated cybersecurity incident.

### Characteristics

* No production systems affected
* Discussion focused
* Decision based
* Facilitator led
* Scenario driven
* Collaborative
* Educational

### Not Intended To

* Test hacking skills
* Conduct penetration testing
* Replace technical incident response drills

### Speaker Notes

Unlike penetration tests or red team exercises, tabletop exercises focus on human decision-making, communication, coordination, and governance. Participants discuss how they would respond rather than executing technical actions on live systems.

# Slide 11 – Understanding Cyber Incident Response

## Learning Objectives

* Define cyber incident response
* Understand why structured response matters
* Recognize the relationship between incident response and TTX

### Key Points

* Incident response is a coordinated approach to managing cyber incidents.
* The goal is to minimize business impact.
* Effective response relies on people, processes, and technology.
* Preparation before an incident is critical.
* Tabletop exercises validate incident response capabilities.

### Speaker Notes

Cyber incident response is the organized process of detecting, analyzing, containing, eradicating, recovering from, and learning from cybersecurity incidents. A mature incident response capability extends beyond technical remediation—it includes executive decision-making, legal considerations, public relations, regulatory reporting, and business continuity.

Tabletop exercises allow organizations to test these processes in a low-risk environment, revealing strengths and weaknesses before a real incident occurs.

### NIST Lifecycle

1. Preparation

3. Containment, Eradication, and Recovery
4. Post-Incident Activity

### Key Discussion

* Preparation is continuous.
* Detection requires visibility.
* Containment balances speed and business impact.

### Speaker Notes

The NIST Computer Security Incident Handling Guide (SP 800-61) provides one of the most widely adopted incident response frameworks. Tabletop exercises should incorporate all four phases to ensure participants understand the complete lifecycle rather than focusing only on technical containment.

# Slide 21 – What is a Cybersecurity Tabletop Exercise?

## Learning Objectives

* Define a cybersecurity tabletop exercise (TTX)
* Understand its purpose
* Differentiate TTX from other cybersecurity assessments

### Key Points

* A discussion-based cybersecurity simulation
* Tests organizational readiness
* Validates policies and procedures
* Focuses on decision-making rather than technical execution
* Conducted in a controlled, low-risk environment
* Encourages collaboration across business units

### Speaker Notes

A Cybersecurity Tabletop Exercise (TTX) is a structured, facilitator-led discussion in which participants respond to a simulated cyber incident. Unlike technical assessments, participants are not expected to operate systems or perform live forensic analysis. Instead, they discuss actions, responsibilities, communication strategies, and decision-making processes. The primary objective is to identify organizational strengths, weaknesses, and opportunities for improvement before a real incident occurs.
