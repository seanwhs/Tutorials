# Slide 101 – Principles of Effective Scenario Design

## Learning Objectives

* Understand the characteristics of a high-quality tabletop scenario
* Align scenarios with exercise objectives
* Balance realism with learning outcomes

### Design Principles

* Risk-driven
* Business-focused
* Intelligence-informed
* Realistic
* Progressive
* Decision-oriented
* Objective-based
* Adaptable

### Characteristics of Effective Scenarios

* Reflect current threats
* Include multiple stakeholders
* Require meaningful decisions
* Test existing plans
* Challenge assumptions
* Encourage collaboration
* Produce measurable outcomes

### Speaker Notes

The scenario is the foundation of every tabletop exercise. A well-designed scenario should reflect the organization's actual threat landscape, business priorities, and operational environment. It should encourage thoughtful discussion without overwhelming participants with unnecessary technical detail. The objective is not to "win" the exercise but to identify opportunities for improvement.

# Slide 121 – What Is an Inject?

## Learning Objectives

* Define an exercise inject
* Understand its role in a tabletop exercise
* Differentiate injects from scenario background

### Definition

An **inject** is a scripted event, piece of information, or simulated communication introduced during a tabletop exercise to advance the scenario, create decision points, and evaluate participant responses.

### Purpose

* Drive discussion
* Test decision-making
* Introduce new information
* Simulate uncertainty
* Maintain exercise momentum
* Validate procedures

### Characteristics of Effective Injects

* Realistic
* Timely
* Objective-driven
* Concise
* Actionable
* Relevant

### Speaker Notes

Injects are the "engine" of a tabletop exercise. While the scenario establishes the context, injects introduce new developments that require participants to assess information, make decisions, and adapt their response. A well-designed inject should challenge participants without revealing the expected answer.

---

# Slide 136 – Facilitator Decision Matrix

## Learning Objectives

* Learn how facilitators decide when to intervene
* Maintain exercise flow while preserving realism

### Purpose

The Facilitator Decision Matrix provides guidance on when to:

* Advance the scenario
* Pause discussion
* Clarify assumptions
* Introduce additional information
* Delay injects
* Accelerate the exercise

### Decision Matrix

| Situation                        | Facilitator Action     |
| -------------------------------- | ---------------------- |
| Discussion has stalled           | Introduce a new inject |
| Participants are confused        | Clarify assumptions    |
| Exercise is behind schedule      | Compress timeline      |
| Participants solve issue quickly | Increase complexity    |
| Team is overloaded               | Pause and summarize    |
| Incorrect assumptions persist    | Provide clarification  |

### Best Practices

* Maintain neutrality.
* Avoid coaching participants.
* Encourage discussion before intervening.
* Keep the exercise aligned with objectives.

### Speaker Notes

The facilitator's role is to guide rather than direct. Interventions should be limited to maintaining exercise flow and ensuring participants remain focused on learning objectives. Allow participants to explore options—even imperfect ones—before providing clarification.
