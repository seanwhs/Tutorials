# Module Overview

The success of a Cybersecurity Tabletop Exercise (TTX) depends long before participants gather in a meeting room. Effective exercises are the result of careful planning, realistic scenario design, clearly defined objectives, stakeholder engagement, logistics preparation, and structured facilitation planning.

Poorly planned exercises often become unfocused discussions with little measurable value. Conversely, well-designed exercises provide meaningful insights into organizational preparedness, validate response capabilities, and generate actionable improvements.

This module introduces the complete planning lifecycle for cybersecurity tabletop exercises, including defining objectives, determining scope, selecting scenarios, engaging stakeholders, developing planning documentation, and preparing for successful execution.

# 2. Tabletop Exercise Planning Lifecycle

A structured planning process ensures consistency and quality.

```text
Initiate
     ↓
Define Objectives
     ↓
Determine Scope
     ↓
Conduct Risk Assessment
     ↓
Select Scenario
     ↓
Identify Participants
     ↓
Develop Exercise Materials
     ↓
Prepare Logistics
     ↓
Conduct Exercise
     ↓
Evaluate
     ↓
Continuous Improvement
```

### Key Principle

Planning should begin several weeks—or even months—before the scheduled exercise, depending on complexity and organizational size.

# 3. Defining Exercise Objectives

Exercise objectives define what success looks like.

### Characteristics of Good Objectives (SMART)

* **Specific**
* **Measurable**
* **Achievable**
* **Relevant**
* **Time-bound**

### Examples

✔ Validate the ransomware incident response plan.

✔ Test executive decision-making during a cyber crisis.

✔ Evaluate communication between IT and Corporate Communications.

✔ Measure escalation time from detection to executive notification.

### Poor Objectives

✘ Improve cybersecurity.

✘ Learn about ransomware.

✘ Practice incident response.

# 5. Determining Exercise Scope

The scope establishes the boundaries of the exercise.

### Scope Considerations

* Business units
* Geographic locations
* Systems included
* Critical assets
* Participants
* External organizations
* Timeframe
* Regulatory requirements

### Example Scope Statement

"This exercise will evaluate the incident response and executive decision-making processes associated with a ransomware attack affecting the organization's primary customer relationship management (CRM) platform."

---

# 6. Identifying Critical Business Processes

Exercises should focus on what matters most.

### Critical Business Functions

* Customer-facing services
* Financial systems
* Manufacturing operations
* Healthcare delivery
* Payment processing
* Cloud services
* Supply chain operations

### Questions

* Which services are mission-critical?
* What is the acceptable downtime?
* Which assets support these services?
* Who owns them?

# 7. Conducting a Risk Assessment

Scenario selection should be informed by organizational risk.

### Risk Assessment Process

1. Identify threats
2. Identify vulnerabilities
3. Assess likelihood
4. Assess business impact
5. Prioritize risks

### Example Risk Matrix

| Threat              | Likelihood | Impact | Priority |
| ------------------- | ---------- | ------ | -------- |
| Ransomware          | High       | High   | Critical |
| Insider Threat      | Medium     | High   | High     |
| DDoS Attack         | Medium     | Medium | Medium   |
| Supply Chain Attack | Low        | High   | Medium   |

---

# 9. Developing Scenario Assumptions

Every exercise should begin with documented assumptions.

### Example Assumptions

* Incident occurs during business hours.
* Internet connectivity remains available.
* Backups exist but their integrity is unknown.
* Key personnel are available.
* Law enforcement has not yet been contacted.

### Benefits

* Reduces ambiguity
* Maintains consistency
* Keeps participants focused

---

# 10. Defining Exercise Constraints

Constraints define what is outside the scope.

### Examples

* No technical system changes.
* No live malware execution.
* No customer notifications.
* No actual media engagement.
* No production system testing.

### Purpose

* Protect operations
* Avoid confusion
* Clarify expectations

# 18. Common Planning Mistakes

Avoid these common pitfalls:

* Unclear objectives
* Overly complex scenarios
* Wrong participants
* Insufficient preparation
* Unrealistic assumptions
* Lack of executive involvement
* No evaluation criteria
* Poor documentation
* No follow-up actions

---

# 19. Planning Best Practices

Successful planners:

* Start early
* Keep objectives focused
* Base scenarios on real risks
* Engage all stakeholders
* Prepare detailed documentation
* Conduct planning meetings
* Validate logistics
* Rehearse facilitator roles
* Plan for evaluation from the outset

# Practical Exercise

## Scenario

You have been appointed as the **Exercise Director** for your organization's first enterprise-wide cybersecurity tabletop exercise.

### Group Activity

Working in teams, develop a complete **Exercise Planning Package** that includes:

1. A clear exercise charter with purpose and scope.
2. Three SMART exercise objectives.
3. A risk assessment identifying the top five cyber threats relevant to your organization.
4. A scenario selection with documented assumptions and constraints.
5. A stakeholder matrix identifying internal and external participants.
6. An 8-week planning timeline with milestones and deliverables.
7. A communications and logistics plan.
8. A planning checklist covering strategic, operational, administrative, and evaluation tasks.

### Deliverable

Present a **20-minute planning briefing** to the class, acting as the Exercise Planning Team. Your presentation should demonstrate how your planning approach aligns with business objectives, supports governance, and establishes the foundation for a successful cybersecurity tabletop exercise.
