# Appendix I.3.5 — Enterprise Incident Response & Crisis Management Assessment Workbook

> *"A resilient organization is not defined by the absence of incidents, but by its ability to make sound decisions under pressure."*

---

# Purpose

Incident response is the operational realization of cyber resilience.

Policies, architectures, security technologies, and training all culminate in one question:

> **Can the organization detect, coordinate, contain, recover, and learn when a significant cyber incident occurs?**

This workbook evaluates the organization's end-to-end incident response capability, including technical operations, executive crisis leadership, communications, legal coordination, and post-incident improvement.

---

# Assessment Scope

The assessment covers:

* Incident response governance.
* Incident classification.
* Incident command.
* Technical response.
* Digital forensics.
* Crisis management.
* Executive decision-making.
* Internal and external communications.
* Legal and regulatory coordination.
* Recovery management.
* Lessons learned and continuous improvement.

---

# Capability Objectives

A mature incident response capability ensures that:

✓ Incidents are rapidly detected, classified, and escalated.

✓ Roles and responsibilities are clearly understood.

✓ Technical responders and business leaders operate within a unified incident command structure.

✓ Decisions are documented and risk-informed.

✓ Communications are timely, accurate, and consistent.

✓ Every incident improves future resilience.

# IR1 — Incident Response Governance

* Incident response policy.
* Response framework.
* Roles and responsibilities.
* Incident response plan.
* Team structure.
* On-call processes.
* Governance reviews.

# IR3 — Incident Command & Coordination

## Objective

Assess the organization's ability to coordinate cross-functional response activities.

### Assessment Areas

* Incident commander role.
* Decision authority.
* Cross-functional participation.
* Situation reporting.
* Resource management.
* Escalation to crisis management.

### Maturity Indicators

**Level 1:** Response coordinated informally.

**Level 3:** Formal incident command established with defined responsibilities.

**Level 5:** Enterprise incident command integrates technical, operational, legal, communications, and executive functions through repeatable governance.

|   Score | Maturity Level | Interpretation                                                                                                                                    |

| 0.0–0.9 | Initial        | Response is largely reactive and dependent on individual expertise.                                                                               |

# Executive Reporting Metrics

Recommended quarterly metrics:

| KPI                                | Description                                                           |
| ---------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Mean Time to Detect (MTTD)         | Average time to identify an incident.                                 |
| Mean Time to Contain (MTTC)        | Average time to stop further impact.                                  |
| Mean Time to Recover (MTTR)        | Average time to restore agreed business services.                     |
| Major Incident Exercises Completed | Executive and cross-functional exercises conducted.                   |
| Corrective Action Closure Rate     | Percentage of post-incident actions completed on schedule.            |
| Repeat Findings                    | Number of recurring issues identified across incidents and exercises. |
