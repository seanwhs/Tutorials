# Appendix I.3.9 — People, Culture & Organizational Resilience Assessment Workbook

> *"Technology enables resilience. People sustain it."*

---

# Purpose

Cyber resilience is ultimately an organizational capability rather than a technological one.

Organizations with similar technologies often experience very different outcomes during major cyber incidents because their cultures differ. Some organizations encourage collaboration, learning, disciplined decision-making, and continuous improvement. Others remain siloed, reactive, and dependent on a few key individuals.

This workbook evaluates whether the organization has developed the leadership, workforce capabilities, organizational culture, and learning mechanisms necessary to sustain cyber resilience over the long term.

---

# Assessment Scope

This workbook evaluates:

* Leadership commitment.
* Organizational culture.
* Workforce capability.
* Role-based competency management.
* Security awareness and behavior.
* Insider risk awareness.
* Cross-functional collaboration.
* Talent management.
* Knowledge management.
* Continuous organizational learning.

---

# Capability Objectives

A mature organizational resilience capability ensures that:

✓ Leadership consistently reinforces resilience as a business priority.

✓ Employees understand their responsibilities during normal operations and crises.

✓ Critical knowledge is shared rather than concentrated in individuals.

✓ Learning from incidents becomes institutional knowledge.

✓ Organizational culture supports transparency, accountability, and continuous improvement.

# PC1 — Leadership Commitment

## Objective

Assess executive sponsorship and leadership engagement.

### Assessment Areas

Evaluate:

* Executive sponsorship.
* Leadership communications.
* Strategic priorities.
* Resource allocation.
* Performance objectives.
* Governance participation.

### Sample Questions

| Question                                                                                | Score |
| --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | :---: |
| Executive leadership visibly supports cyber resilience initiatives.                     |       |
| Leaders regularly communicate resilience objectives.                                    |       |
| Cyber resilience responsibilities are reflected in leadership performance goals.        |       |
| Senior leaders participate in major exercises.                                          |       |
| Leadership decisions reinforce long-term resilience rather than short-term convenience. |       |

---

# PC2 — Organizational Culture

## Objective

Determine whether the organizational culture encourages resilience.

### Assessment Areas

Evaluate:

* Psychological safety.
* Reporting culture.
* Accountability.
* Collaboration.
* Continuous improvement.
* Ethical behavior.

### Common Weaknesses

* Blame-oriented post-incident reviews.
* Siloed decision-making.
* Reluctance to escalate emerging risks.
* Limited collaboration between business and technology teams.

---

# PC3 — Workforce Capability

## Objective

Assess whether personnel possess the knowledge and skills required to support resilience.

### Assessment Areas

Evaluate:

* Workforce planning.
* Skills inventories.
* Professional development.
* Certification programs.
* Technical and non-technical competencies.
* Contractor capability.

### Sample Questions

| Question                                                           | Score |
| ------------------------------------------------------------------ | :---: |
| Critical resilience roles have documented competency requirements. |       |
| Skills gaps are periodically assessed.                             |       |
| Employees receive role-appropriate training.                       |       |
| Workforce development plans align with future business needs.      |       |
| External contractors receive appropriate onboarding.               |       |

# PC4 — Role-Based Competency Management

## Objective

Evaluate whether individuals understand and are prepared for their resilience responsibilities.

### Assessment Areas

Assess:

* Role definitions.
* Competency frameworks.
* Crisis responsibilities.
* Executive preparedness.
* Technical specialization.
* Cross-training.

### Evidence Examples

* Role descriptions.
* Competency matrices.
* Training records.
* Exercise participation logs.

---

# PC5 — Security Awareness & Human Behavior

## Objective

Assess how effectively the organization influences secure behaviors.

### Assessment Areas

Evaluate:

* Awareness programs.
* Phishing simulations.
* Secure working practices.
* Behavioral reinforcement.
* Metrics and effectiveness reviews.
* Executive participation.

### Maturity Indicators

**Level 1:** Annual compliance-focused awareness training.

**Level 3:** Continuous role-based awareness supported by behavioral metrics.

**Level 5:** Security-conscious behaviors are embedded into daily work practices and reinforced through leadership, coaching, and measurable outcomes.

---

# PC6 — Insider Risk Awareness

## Objective

Assess the organization's ability to reduce risks associated with trusted users.

### Assessment Areas

Evaluate:

* Insider risk governance.
* Privileged user awareness.
* Behavioral monitoring.
* Separation of duties.
* Reporting mechanisms.
* HR and legal coordination.

### Sample Questions

| Question                                                         | Score |
| ---------------------------------------------------------------- | :---: |
| Insider risk scenarios are incorporated into awareness programs. |       |
| High-risk roles receive enhanced monitoring and support.         |       |
| Reporting channels are trusted and well understood.              |       |
| Insider risk is coordinated across HR, Legal, and Security.      |       |
| Lessons from insider events inform policy updates.               |       |

---

# PC8 — Knowledge Management

## Objective

Assess preservation and sharing of organizational knowledge.

### Assessment Areas

Evaluate:

* Lessons learned repositories.
* Standard operating procedures.
* Documentation quality.
* Knowledge transfer.
* Communities of practice.
* Runbooks and playbooks.

### Common Weaknesses

* Critical knowledge concentrated in a few individuals.
* Outdated procedures.
* Limited documentation of operational experience.

# Executive Interpretation

|   Score | Maturity Level | Interpretation                                                                                                                                                                  |
| ------: | -------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 0.0–0.9 | Initial        | Resilience depends on individual effort rather than organizational capability.                                                                                                  |
| 1.0–1.9 | Developing     | Awareness and training programs exist but are inconsistent and siloed.                                                                                                          |
| 2.0–2.9 | Defined        | Roles, competencies, and learning processes are standardized across the enterprise.                                                                                             |
| 3.0–3.9 | Managed        | Organizational culture actively supports collaboration, accountability, and continuous improvement.                                                                             |
| 4.0–5.0 | Adaptive       | Resilience is embedded in leadership behaviors, workforce capabilities, and organizational culture, enabling continuous adaptation to changing threats and business conditions. |
