# Appendix I.3.6 — Recovery, Business Continuity & Operational Resilience Assessment Workbook

> *"Recovery is not the objective. The objective is sustaining critical business services despite disruption."*

---

# Purpose

Recovery is often misunderstood as restoring servers, applications, or data.

Enterprise resilience requires a broader objective:

**Restore the organization's ability to deliver critical business services within agreed operational tolerances.**

Technology recovery is one component of that objective.

Business continuity, operational resilience, crisis leadership, third-party coordination, and recovery governance are equally important.

This workbook evaluates the organization's ability to:

* Recover technology.
* Continue essential business operations.
* Restore critical services.
* Coordinate enterprise recovery.
* Validate operational readiness before resuming normal operations.
* Learn from recovery activities.

---

This assessment evaluates:

* Recovery governance.
* Business Impact Analysis (BIA).
* Recovery strategy.
* Disaster recovery.
* Backup resilience.
* Business continuity planning.
* Operational resilience.
* Crisis recovery governance.
* Third-party recovery.
* Continuous recovery improvement.

---

# Capability Objectives

A mature recovery capability ensures that:

✓ Critical business services are identified and prioritized.

✓ Recovery objectives are defined and validated.

✓ Recovery decisions are governed by business risk rather than technical convenience.

✓ Recovery capabilities are exercised regularly.

✓ Recovery performance is measured and improved.

# RC2 — Business Impact Analysis (BIA)

## Objective

Determine whether recovery priorities are based on business impact.

### Assessment Areas

Evaluate:

* Critical business services.
* Maximum tolerable disruption.
* Recovery Time Objectives (RTOs).
* Recovery Point Objectives (RPOs).
* Dependency mapping.
* Business process criticality.

### Evidence Examples

* BIA reports.
* Service dependency maps.
* Recovery priority lists.
* Process inventories.

### Common Weaknesses

* Outdated BIAs.
* Technology-focused recovery priorities.
* Incomplete dependency mapping.

# RC5 — Backup & Data Protection

## Objective

Assess resilience of backup and restoration capabilities.

### Assessment Areas

Evaluate:

* Backup coverage.
* Immutable backups.
* Offline backup strategies.
* Encryption.
* Backup monitoring.
* Restoration testing.
* Backup integrity validation.

### Maturity Indicators

**Level 1:** Backups exist but restoration is rarely tested.

**Level 3:** Regular restoration testing validates recovery objectives.

**Level 5:** Immutable, diversified, continuously validated backup architecture with automated integrity verification.
