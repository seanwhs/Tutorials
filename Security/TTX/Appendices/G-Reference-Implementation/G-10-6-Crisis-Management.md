# G.10.6 Master Scenario Events List (MSEL)

## Phase 5 – Crisis Management

> *"Technical recovery restores systems. Crisis leadership preserves trust."*

---

# Phase Overview

The incident is now publicly visible. While the Incident Response Team continues technical containment and forensic investigation, executive leadership must address increasing pressure from customers, employees, suppliers, regulators, media, and the Board of Directors.

This phase evaluates how effectively the organization manages communications, governance, legal obligations, and strategic decision-making while maintaining confidence among internal and external stakeholders.

The White Cell should increase the tempo slightly, introducing competing priorities and interruptions that reflect the reality of a major organizational crisis.

---

# Phase Objectives

By the conclusion of Phase 5, participants should have:

* Activated formal crisis management governance.
* Coordinated executive communications.
* Managed internal and external messaging.
* Considered legal and regulatory obligations.
* Maintained alignment between technical and executive teams.
* Demonstrated disciplined decision-making under sustained pressure.

# RH-INJ-041 — Media Inquiry

| Field    | Details                              |
| -------- | ------------------------------------ |
| Time     | T+180                                |
| Delivery | Email from Journalist                |
| Audience | Communications, Executive Leadership |
| Artifact | ART-022 – Journalist Inquiry         |

### Inject Content

A journalist contacts Corporate Communications requesting confirmation of reports that the organization is responding to a cybersecurity incident affecting manufacturing operations.

A response is requested within two hours.

### Expected Discussion

* Should the organization respond?
* What information has been verified?
* Who is authorized to speak publicly?

### Expected Actions

* Prepare a holding statement.
* Coordinate with Legal.
* Brief executive leadership.

### Observer Focus

* Message discipline.
* Governance.
* Approval workflow.

# Phase 5 Facilitator Review

Before progressing, confirm that participants have:

* Managed communications across internal and external stakeholders.
* Briefed executive leadership and the Board.
* Considered legal and regulatory obligations.
* Coordinated customer, supplier, and employee communications.
* Established governance for sustained crisis management.
* Maintained alignment between technical operations and executive decision-making.

If governance or communications remain fragmented, use targeted discussion before introducing recovery activities.

---

# Key Learning Outcomes

Phase 5 reinforces that successful crisis management depends on trust, coordination, and disciplined leadership.

Participants should recognize that:

* Communication should be timely, accurate, and proportionate.
* Executive leaders must communicate uncertainty without creating unnecessary alarm.
* Legal, Communications, HR, Customer Success, and Security must operate as a unified leadership team.
* Governance structures are essential for maintaining decision quality during prolonged incidents.
* Stakeholder confidence can be preserved even when technical certainty is incomplete.

---

# Transition to Phase 6

The immediate crisis has stabilized.

Attention now turns toward:

* Restoring critical business services.
* Validating system integrity.
* Confirming that the threat has been contained.
* Returning to normal operations safely.
* Capturing lessons learned.
* Building long-term organizational resilience.

The final phase asks the question that ultimately determines the success of every incident response:

**"How do we recover stronger than before?"**
