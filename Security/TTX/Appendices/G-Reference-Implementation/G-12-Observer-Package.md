# G.12 Observer Package

## Operation Red Horizon

> *"Observers do not judge decisions. They capture how decisions are made."*

---

# Document Purpose

This package defines the role of observers during Operation Red Horizon.

Observers support the exercise by capturing:

* Participant decisions.
* Communication patterns.
* Coordination effectiveness.
* Process strengths.
* Capability gaps.
* Improvement opportunities.

Observer notes become a primary input into:

* Hot Wash discussion.
* After Action Review.
* Improvement roadmap.

---

# 1. Observer Role

## Primary Responsibility

The observer's role is to:

> Capture evidence of organizational behaviour during the exercise.

Observers should focus on:

* What happened.
* When it happened.
* Who was involved.
* What information was available.
* What decisions were made.
* What outcomes occurred.

---

# 2. Observer Responsibilities

Observers should:

## Record Facts

Example:

Good observation:

> "At T+45, SOC notified Incident Commander approximately 15 minutes after confirming suspicious authentication activity."

Poor observation:

> "SOC was too slow."

---

## Capture Decisions

Record:

* Decision made.
* Decision owner.
* Information available.
* Alternatives considered.
* Outcome.

---

## Identify Patterns

Look for:

* Repeated communication delays.
* Unclear ownership.
* Missing procedures.
* Dependency issues.

---

## Remain Silent During Exercise

Observers should:

* Listen.
* Record.
* Avoid coaching.

Observers should not:

* Correct participants.
* Provide recommendations during the exercise.
* Influence decisions.

---

# 3. Observation Principles

## Observe Behaviour, Not Personality

Avoid:

> "The manager did not understand security."

Record:

> "Business leadership requested confirmation of customer impact before approving external communication."

---

## Capture Context

Every observation should answer:

* What happened?
* Why did it happen?
* What impact did it create?

---

## Separate Facts From Interpretation

Example:

Fact:

> "No owner was assigned for customer communication."

Interpretation:

> "Customer communication process lacks clear ownership."

Both are useful, but they should remain separate.

---

# 4. Observation Framework

Observers should assess five capability domains.

---

# Domain 1 — Detection and Analysis

## Objective

Evaluate how effectively the organization identifies and understands the incident.

---

## Observe

| Area                | Evidence                          |
| ------------------- | --------------------------------- |
| Alert handling      | How quickly alerts were reviewed  |
| Investigation       | Quality of analysis               |
| Information sharing | Flow between teams                |
| Escalation          | Appropriateness of response level |

---

## Questions

* Were indicators correlated?
* Was additional information requested?
* Were assumptions documented?

---

# Domain 2 — Incident Coordination

## Objective

Evaluate response organization.

---

## Observe

| Area             | Evidence               |
| ---------------- | ---------------------- |
| Incident command | Clear leadership       |
| Roles            | Ownership clarity      |
| Meetings         | Effective coordination |
| Decisions        | Timely approval        |

---

## Questions

* Was an Incident Commander established?
* Did teams understand responsibilities?
* Were decisions tracked?

---

# Domain 3 — Business Integration

## Objective

Evaluate whether cyber response considered business priorities.

---

## Observe

| Area          | Evidence                   |
| ------------- | -------------------------- |
| Operations    | Business impact assessment |
| Manufacturing | OT considerations          |
| Customers     | External impact            |
| Finance       | Cost considerations        |

---

## Questions

* Were business leaders involved early?
* Were operational risks discussed?
* Were recovery priorities clear?

---

# Domain 4 — Communication

## Objective

Evaluate internal and external information flow.

---

## Observe

| Area                   | Evidence                 |
| ---------------------- | ------------------------ |
| Internal communication | Employee awareness       |
| Executive reporting    | Leadership briefings     |
| External communication | Customer/media readiness |

---

## Questions

* Was communication timely?
* Were facts separated from assumptions?
* Were messages coordinated?

---

# Domain 5 — Recovery and Improvement

## Objective

Evaluate organizational learning.

---

## Observe

| Area              | Evidence                   |
| ----------------- | -------------------------- |
| Recovery planning | Restoration approach       |
| Validation        | Security checks            |
| Lessons learned   | Improvement identification |

---

## Questions

* Were recovery decisions documented?
* Were improvements assigned owners?
* Did teams identify systemic issues?

# 8. Common Observer Mistakes

Observers should avoid:

---

## Mistake 1 — Judging Instead of Recording

Incorrect:

> "Leadership failed."

Better:

> "Leadership requested additional information before approving customer notification."

---

## Mistake 2 — Focusing Only on Technology

Cyber incidents involve:

* People.
* Processes.
* Governance.
* Business operations.

---

## Mistake 3 — Recording Only Failures

Capture:

* What worked.
* What should continue.
* What should improve.

---

## Mistake 4 — Providing Solutions During Exercise

Observers collect evidence.

Recommendations belong in:

* Hot Wash.
* AAR.
* Improvement roadmap.

---

# 9. Observer Hot Wash Preparation

Before the Hot Wash, observers should consolidate:

## Top Strengths

Examples:

* Rapid escalation.
* Effective teamwork.
* Clear leadership.

---

## Top Challenges

Examples:

* Unclear ownership.
* Communication delays.
* Missing procedures.

---

## Top Recommendations

Examples:

* Update escalation matrix.
* Improve third-party access controls.
* Test recovery procedures.

---

# 10. Observer Checklist

Before Exercise

☐ Understand scenario objectives.
☐ Review observation framework.
☐ Prepare note-taking method.
☐ Confirm assigned team.

---

During Exercise

☐ Record timestamps.
☐ Capture decisions.
☐ Record evidence.
☐ Avoid intervention.

---

After Exercise

☐ Consolidate notes.
☐ Identify themes.
☐ Prepare Hot Wash input.
☐ Support AAR development.

---

# Final Observer Message

A tabletop exercise creates value only when lessons are captured.

The observer's responsibility is to transform:

**Conversation → Evidence → Findings → Improvement**

The quality of the final roadmap depends directly on the quality of observation.
