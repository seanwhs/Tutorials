# G.7 Participant Guide

> *"Come prepared to think, question, collaborate, and make decisions—not to find the 'right' answer."*

---

# Welcome

Thank you for participating in **Operation Red Horizon**, ACME Manufacturing Group's enterprise cybersecurity tabletop exercise.

Your participation is an important part of the organization's cybersecurity resilience program. The exercise provides an opportunity to explore how we would collectively respond to a complex cyber incident, evaluate our current plans and processes, and identify practical improvements before a real incident occurs.

Operation Red Horizon is a learning exercise. It is designed to strengthen organizational preparedness through discussion, collaboration, and informed decision-making.

---

# Purpose of the Exercise

Cybersecurity incidents rarely affect only technical teams.

A significant cyber event may require coordinated decisions involving executive leadership, legal counsel, manufacturing operations, communications, human resources, supply chain management, business continuity, and information technology.

The purpose of this exercise is to examine how these functions work together under realistic conditions while balancing technical, operational, legal, financial, and reputational considerations.

---

# Learning Objectives

By participating in this exercise, you will have the opportunity to:

* Practice cross-functional collaboration.
* Evaluate incident response processes.
* Improve executive communication.
* Examine business continuity decisions.
* Discuss third-party cyber risk.
* Validate escalation procedures.
* Explore recovery priorities.
* Identify opportunities for organizational improvement.

The objective is organizational learning rather than individual evaluation.

---

# What This Exercise Is

Operation Red Horizon is a facilitated discussion-based tabletop exercise.

Participants will:

* Review evolving information.
* Discuss possible actions.
* Explain decisions.
* Request additional information.
* Coordinate with other business functions.
* Respond to changing circumstances.

No production systems will be accessed, and no technical actions will be performed.

---

# What This Exercise Is Not

This exercise is **not**:

* A penetration test.
* A red team engagement.
* A digital forensic investigation.
* A compliance audit.
* An individual performance assessment.
* A certification examination.

The emphasis is on decision-making and collaboration.

---

# Your Role

Please participate as though you are responding to an actual organizational incident.

When making decisions:

* Base them on information available at the time.
* Follow existing organizational policies where appropriate.
* Explain your reasoning.
* Raise concerns openly.
* Ask questions whenever additional information would normally be required.

Do not attempt to "solve the exercise."

Instead, focus on making the best decisions possible with the information available.

---

# Exercise Principles

Several principles guide the exercise.

## Realism

The scenario has been designed to reflect realistic organizational conditions.

Although fictional, the events are representative of incidents experienced by many organizations.

---

## Collaboration

No single participant possesses all the information needed to understand the evolving situation.

Effective collaboration is therefore essential.

---

## Uncertainty

Information will emerge gradually.

Some details may be incomplete.

Some assumptions may later prove incorrect.

Managing uncertainty is an intentional part of the learning experience.

---

## Continuous Learning

Participants are encouraged to:

* Share experience.
* Challenge assumptions respectfully.
* Discuss alternative approaches.
* Reflect on organizational processes.

There are no penalties for proposing different viewpoints.

---

# Participant Expectations

Participants are expected to:

* Arrive on time.
* Review the distributed materials.
* Participate actively.
* Listen respectfully.
* Explain key decisions.
* Collaborate across organizational boundaries.
* Remain engaged throughout the exercise.

Different perspectives are encouraged.

Constructive disagreement often produces valuable learning.

---

# Information Requests

During the exercise you may request information that would normally be available to your role.

Examples include:

* Security alerts.
* System status.
* Executive updates.
* Legal advice.
* Vendor communications.
* Customer impact information.
* Network diagrams.
* Asset inventories.

The White Cell will determine what information is available based on the scenario timeline.

---

# Decision-Making Guidance

As the exercise develops, consider questions such as:

* What do we know?
* What do we assume?
* What remains uncertain?
* What business services are affected?
* Who needs to be informed?
* Which decisions are time critical?
* What are the potential consequences of action—or inaction?

These questions often matter more than the technical details.

---

# Communication During the Exercise

Use clear and concise communication.

When presenting recommendations:

* State the known facts.
* Identify assumptions.
* Explain the risks.
* Recommend a course of action.
* Highlight any decisions requiring executive approval.

Avoid unnecessary technical jargon when communicating with business stakeholders.

---

# Psychological Safety

Operation Red Horizon is conducted under a formal no-blame philosophy.

Participants are encouraged to:

* Ask questions.
* Admit uncertainty.
* Change their minds when new evidence emerges.
* Learn from differing viewpoints.

Mistakes made during discussion are valuable learning opportunities.

---

# After the Exercise

Following completion of the exercise:

* A facilitated Hot Wash will capture immediate observations.
* Observers will consolidate their notes.
* The planning team will prepare an After Action Review (AAR).
* Improvement opportunities will be prioritized and assigned to owners.
* Progress will be tracked through the organization's resilience improvement program.

Participant feedback plays an important role in shaping future exercises.

---

# Preparation Checklist

Before joining the exercise, please ensure that you have:

* Reviewed the Exercise Charter (recommended).
* Read the Rules of Engagement.
* Confirmed your role in the exercise.
* Familiarized yourself with applicable organizational procedures.
* Tested your meeting technology (if participating remotely).
* Reserved sufficient time to participate without interruption.

Preparation enables more productive discussion.

---

# Frequently Asked Questions

### Will I be assessed?

No.

The exercise evaluates organizational processes rather than individual performance.

---

### What if I don't know the answer?

Ask questions.

Incident response is a collaborative process, and uncertainty is expected.

---

### Can I propose alternative approaches?

Yes.

Exploring multiple options is encouraged.

---

### What if I think the scenario is unrealistic?

Raise the concern during discussion.

The facilitator may clarify assumptions or explain the design intent.

---

### Can we pause the exercise?

Yes.

The facilitator may invoke a PAUSE to clarify issues or address participant concerns.

---

# Final Message

The value of Operation Red Horizon does not depend on making perfect decisions.

Its value lies in creating meaningful conversations that improve organizational preparedness before a real incident occurs.

Approach the exercise with curiosity, professionalism, and a willingness to learn from colleagues across the organization.

Together, these discussions strengthen not only cybersecurity capabilities but the organization's overall resilience.
