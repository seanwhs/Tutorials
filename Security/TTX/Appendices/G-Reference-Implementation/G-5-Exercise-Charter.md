# Executive Summary

Operation Red Horizon is a strategic, discussion-based cybersecurity tabletop exercise designed to evaluate ACME Manufacturing Group's ability to respond to a significant cyber incident originating from the compromise of a trusted third-party vendor.

The exercise examines the organization's capability to detect suspicious activity, coordinate cross-functional decision-making, protect critical business services, communicate effectively with internal and external stakeholders, and recover safely while maintaining operational resilience.

Rather than evaluating technical proficiency alone, Operation Red Horizon focuses on leadership, governance, communication, coordination, and organizational decision-making under conditions of uncertainty.

---

# Exercise Purpose

The primary purpose of this exercise is to strengthen enterprise resilience through structured discussion and collaborative decision-making.

The exercise seeks to validate that existing plans, procedures, governance structures, and communication mechanisms enable the organization to respond effectively to a complex cybersecurity incident affecting multiple business functions.

The exercise is intended to identify opportunities for improvement—not to evaluate individual performance.

---

# Strategic Objectives

Operation Red Horizon has six strategic objectives.

## Objective 1 — Validate Incident Response Governance

Confirm that roles, responsibilities, escalation paths, and decision authorities are understood and executed appropriately.

---

## Objective 2 — Evaluate Cross-Functional Coordination

Assess collaboration between Cybersecurity, IT Operations, Legal, Communications, Human Resources, Manufacturing, Supply Chain, and Executive Leadership.

---

## Objective 3 — Assess Business Decision-Making

Evaluate how technical findings are translated into business decisions relating to operational continuity, customer impact, legal obligations, and reputational risk.

---

## Objective 4 — Examine Third-Party Risk Response

Assess the organization's ability to manage cybersecurity incidents involving trusted vendors and external partners.

---

## Objective 5 — Validate Crisis Communications

Review executive reporting, stakeholder communications, media handling, customer messaging, and regulatory notification processes.

---

## Objective 6 — Improve Organizational Resilience

Generate actionable findings that strengthen future preparedness through policy improvements, technology enhancements, training, and governance refinement.

# Exercise Scope

The exercise focuses on enterprise-level coordination rather than technical investigation.

### In Scope

* Incident detection and triage.
* Executive escalation.
* Identity compromise.
* Third-party risk management.
* Cross-functional coordination.
* Manufacturing continuity.
* Crisis communications.
* Legal and regulatory considerations.
* Business continuity.
* Executive decision-making.
* Recovery planning.

---

### Out of Scope

The following activities are intentionally excluded:

* Live production system changes.
* Technical penetration testing.
* Malware execution.
* Digital forensic validation.
* Real customer notifications.
* Live regulator engagement.
* Infrastructure recovery testing.
* Business continuity activation in production.

These exclusions ensure the exercise remains discussion-based and safe to conduct.

---

# Exercise Assumptions

To maintain focus, the planning team has agreed upon the following assumptions.

* Existing cybersecurity policies are available and approved.
* Incident Response Plans have been previously documented.
* Business Continuity Plans exist.
* Disaster Recovery Plans exist.
* Participants understand their normal organizational responsibilities.
* Technical systems described in the scenario represent the current enterprise environment.
* All exercise events are fictional unless the facilitator explicitly pauses the exercise.

Participants should avoid debating whether a policy exists unless the scenario specifically introduces that uncertainty.

---

# Exercise Constraints

Several practical constraints apply.

* The exercise duration is limited to four hours.
* No production systems will be accessed.
* All artifacts are simulated.
* White Cell decisions are authoritative.
* Facilitators may accelerate or compress time where appropriate.
* Some technical details will be simplified to maintain business focus.

---

# Success Criteria

The exercise will be considered successful if participants:

* Detect the evolving incident through collaborative discussion.
* Escalate appropriately using existing governance structures.
* Demonstrate effective communication across organizational boundaries.
* Balance business continuity with cybersecurity risk.
* Make timely and proportionate containment decisions.
* Identify meaningful organizational improvements.

Success is measured by organizational learning rather than operational perfection.

# Risk Management

Although discussion-based exercises present minimal operational risk, several safeguards apply.

Potential risks include:

* Confusion with real-world incidents.
* Accidental disclosure of sensitive information.
* Emotional stress resulting from realistic scenarios.
* Confing with concurrent operational activities.
* Misinterpretation of exercise communications.

Mitigation measures include:

* Clear Rules of Engagement.
* Exercise-specific communication prefixes.
* White Cell oversight.
* STOP and PAUSE procedures.
* Sanitized artifacts.
* Executive awareness.
