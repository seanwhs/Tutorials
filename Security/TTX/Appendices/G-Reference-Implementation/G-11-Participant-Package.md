# G.11 Participant Package

## Operation Red Horizon

> *A cyber incident tabletop exercise focused on enterprise resilience, decision-making, and coordinated response.*

---

# Document Purpose

This package provides participants with the information required to effectively participate in Operation Red Horizon.

It explains:

* Exercise purpose.
* Participant expectations.
* Exercise rules.
* Roles and responsibilities.
* Communication methods.
* Decision-making expectations.

This document intentionally does **not** contain:

* Future injects.
* Scenario progression.
* Expected responses.
* Facilitator notes.
* Hidden exercise assumptions.

---

# 1. Exercise Overview

## Exercise Name

**Operation Red Horizon**

---

## Exercise Type

Enterprise Cybersecurity Tabletop Exercise (TTX)

---

## Exercise Format

Discussion-based simulation involving:

* Cybersecurity teams.
* IT operations.
* Business leadership.
* Manufacturing operations.
* Legal.
* Communications.
* Human Resources.
* Supply Chain.
* Executive leadership.

---

## Scenario Theme

The organization is responding to a suspected cybersecurity incident involving unauthorized access through a trusted third-party relationship.

The exercise explores how the organization:

* Detects suspicious activity.
* Coordinates response.
* Protects business operations.
* Communicates with stakeholders.
* Recovers from disruption.
* Improves future resilience.

---

# 2. Exercise Objectives

The purpose of Operation Red Horizon is not to test individual technical skills.

The purpose is to evaluate organizational capability.

Participants should demonstrate the ability to:

## Detect and Assess

* Recognize indicators of compromise.
* Validate information.
* Determine escalation requirements.

---

## Coordinate Response

* Work across organizational boundaries.
* Share information effectively.
* Assign clear ownership.

---

## Make Decisions Under Uncertainty

* Evaluate incomplete information.
* Balance risk and business priorities.
* Document assumptions.

---

## Manage Business Impact

* Protect critical operations.
* Consider customer and supplier impacts.
* Maintain organizational trust.

---

## Improve Resilience

* Identify capability gaps.
* Recommend improvements.
* Strengthen future response.

---

# 3. Exercise Rules

## This Is a No-Blame Exercise

Operation Red Horizon is designed for learning.

The objective is not to identify:

* Who made mistakes.
* Which team performed poorly.
* Which individual made the wrong decision.

The objective is to understand:

* Where processes work.
* Where processes fail.
* How the organization can improve.

---

# 4. Assume Good Intent

Participants should assume:

* Information provided is realistic.
* Teams are acting professionally.
* Decisions are made with available information.

Do not criticize decisions using information that was not available at the time.

---

# 5. Exercise Reality Rules

During the exercise:

## Treat Scenario Information as Real

When an inject is introduced, assume:

* The event has occurred.
* The information is accurate unless stated otherwise.

---

## Do Not Invent Capabilities

Participants should not assume:

* Tools that do not exist.
* Personnel who are unavailable.
* Processes that are undocumented.

If uncertain, state:

> "In a real incident, we would verify whether this capability exists."

---

## Ask Questions

Participants are encouraged to ask:

* What information is available?
* Who owns this decision?
* What constraints exist?
* What risks should be considered?

---

# 6. Decision-Making Expectations

During the exercise, participants should:

## State Decisions Clearly

Example:

> "We recommend disabling the vendor account immediately because the risk of continued unauthorized access exceeds the operational impact."

---

## Identify Assumptions

Example:

> "We are assuming the compromised account has not accessed production systems."

---

## Identify Owners

Every major action should have:

* Responsible person.
* Decision authority.
* Expected timeframe.

---

## Explain Trade-offs

Good decisions include discussion of:

* Benefits.
* Risks.
* Alternatives.

---

# 7. Communication Guidelines

All exercise communications should follow professional incident response practices.

Participants should:

* Share relevant information quickly.
* Avoid speculation.
* Distinguish facts from assumptions.
* Identify confidence levels.

---

## Recommended Language

Use:

> "Current evidence indicates..."

instead of:

> "The attacker definitely..."

---

Use:

> "We assess this as likely..."

instead of:

> "This is confirmed..."

---

# 8. Exercise Roles

## Incident Commander

Primary responsibilities:

* Coordinate response.
* Establish priorities.
* Make escalation decisions.
* Maintain situational awareness.

---

## Security Operations / SOC

Responsibilities:

* Analyze alerts.
* Identify suspicious activity.
* Provide technical findings.

---

## IT Operations

Responsibilities:

* Assess infrastructure impact.
* Support containment.
* Coordinate restoration.

---

## Enterprise Architecture / Security Architecture

Responsibilities:

* Evaluate systemic risk.
* Recommend technical strategies.
* Identify control gaps.

---

## Manufacturing / OT Operations

Responsibilities:

* Protect operational continuity.
* Assess production impact.
* Balance safety and availability.

---

## Business Leadership

Responsibilities:

* Prioritize business needs.
* Accept or reject operational risk.
* Support strategic decisions.

---

## Legal and Compliance

Responsibilities:

* Assess obligations.
* Provide governance guidance.
* Support communications decisions.

---

## Communications

Responsibilities:

* Coordinate messaging.
* Manage stakeholder communication.
* Protect organizational reputation.

---

## Human Resources

Responsibilities:

* Manage employee communication.
* Support workforce coordination.

---

## Supply Chain / Procurement

Responsibilities:

* Manage supplier relationships.
* Assess third-party impact.

---

# 10. Participant Behaviour Expectations

Participants should:

## Do

✓ Discuss openly.
✓ Challenge assumptions.
✓ Consider business impact.
✓ Ask difficult questions.
✓ Record decisions.

---

## Avoid

✗ Searching for the "correct answer."
✗ Defending existing processes.
✗ Blaming individuals.
✗ Ignoring business consequences.
✗ Assuming technology solves everything.

# 14. Exercise Success Criteria

Operation Red Horizon is considered successful when participants:

* Communicate effectively.
* Make decisions transparently.
* Understand dependencies.
* Identify improvement opportunities.
* Strengthen organizational resilience.

Success does **not** require:

* Perfect decisions.
* Immediate resolution.
* Complete technical certainty.

---

# Final Participant Message

Cyber incidents are rarely solved by one team.

Successful response requires:

* Technology.
* Leadership.
* Communication.
* Governance.
* Business understanding.

Operation Red Horizon provides a safe environment to practice these capabilities before they are needed in reality.
