# G.13 Hot Wash Package

## Operation Red Horizon

> *"The best time to capture lessons is immediately after the decision has been made."*

---

# 1. Purpose

The Hot Wash is an immediate discussion conducted after completion of the exercise.

Its purpose is to capture:

* Participant reactions.
* Initial observations.
* Confusion points.
* Decision challenges.
* Process improvement opportunities.

The Hot Wash is **not** the final evaluation.

It is the raw input for:

* Observer consolidation.
* After Action Review.
* Improvement roadmap.

---

# 2. Hot Wash Principles

## Psychological Safety

Participants should feel safe to discuss:

* What worked.
* What failed.
* What was unclear.
* What caused difficulty.

The objective is organizational improvement, not individual assessment.

---

## Capture Reality, Not Perfection

A realistic incident response includes:

* Missing information.
* Conflicting priorities.
* Unclear ownership.
* Difficult decisions.

These are valuable discoveries.

---

## Separate Experience From Solution

During Hot Wash:

Capture:

> "The vendor escalation process was unclear."

Later AAR:

Determine:

> "Update third-party incident escalation procedures."

---

# 3. Hot Wash Timing

Recommended duration:

| Exercise Size                          | Duration      |
| -------------------------------------- | ------------- |
| Small team (<15 participants)          | 30 minutes    |
| Medium exercise (15–40 participants)   | 45–60 minutes |
| Enterprise exercise (>40 participants) | 60–90 minutes |

---

# 4. Facilitator Opening Script

The facilitator should begin:

> "Thank you everyone for participating in Operation Red Horizon. The purpose of this discussion is to capture your immediate observations while the experience is still fresh. This is a learning activity, not an evaluation of individuals or teams."

---

> "There are no perfect answers in a tabletop exercise. We are interested in understanding how decisions were made, what information was available, and where the organization can improve."

---

# 5. Hot Wash Structure

The discussion should follow five sections:

1. Overall Experience
2. Detection and Response
3. Decision-Making
4. Communication and Coordination
5. Improvement Opportunities

---

# 6. Section 1 — Overall Experience

## Facilitator Questions

Ask:

### What surprised you most?

Examples:

* Unexpected dependencies.
* Missing information.
* Coordination challenges.

---

### What felt realistic?

Capture:

* Scenario quality.
* Business relevance.
* Decision pressure.

---

### What felt unrealistic?

Capture:

* Assumptions.
* Missing constraints.
* Areas requiring adjustment.

# 8. Section 3 — Decision-Making

## Facilitator Questions

Ask:

### Which decisions were the hardest?

Examples:

* Isolation versus availability.
* Customer communication.
* Restoration timing.

---

### What information would have improved decisions?

Capture:

* Missing data.
* Delayed reports.
* Unclear ownership.

---

### Were decision authorities clear?

Discuss:

* Who could approve actions?
* Who needed escalation?
* Who accepted risk?

# 14. Hot Wash Output

The Hot Wash should produce:

## Immediate Observations

Examples:

* Escalation paths unclear.
* Recovery priorities inconsistent.

---

## Initial Strengths

Examples:

* Strong technical collaboration.
* Effective executive engagement.

---

## Initial Improvement Themes

Examples:

* Third-party access governance.
* Incident communication process.
* Recovery testing.

---

# 15. Transition to AAR

The Hot Wash provides raw observations.

The next step is structured analysis.

The AAR will transform:

```
Experience
    ↓
Observation
    ↓
Finding
    ↓
Recommendation
    ↓
Owner
    ↓
Improvement Roadmap
```

---

# Final Hot Wash Message

A successful tabletop does not end when the scenario ends.

The value comes from understanding:

* How decisions were made.
* Where friction occurred.
* Which capabilities need improvement.

The Hot Wash captures the organization's immediate learning before it fades.
