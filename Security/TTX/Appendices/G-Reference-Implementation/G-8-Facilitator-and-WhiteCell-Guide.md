# G.8 Facilitator & White Cell Guide

> *"Participants experience the exercise. Facilitators orchestrate the learning."*

---

# Document Purpose

This guide provides the operational instructions required to plan, deliver, and control Operation Red Horizon.

It is intended exclusively for members of the exercise management team and contains information that must not be disclosed to participants before or during the exercise.

The guide establishes a common understanding of:

* Exercise intent.
* Scenario truth.
* Facilitation strategy.
* White Cell responsibilities.
* Inject management.
* Decision adjudication.
* Exercise pacing.
* Observation priorities.
* Contingency procedures.

Used consistently, it enables different facilitators to deliver a comparable learning experience while preserving realism and flexibility.

---

# Confidentiality

This document contains:

* Hidden scenario details.
* Expected participant actions.
* Inject sequencing.
* Facilitator prompts.
* White Cell decision rules.
* Exercise contingency plans.

It must not be shared with participants.

Questions from participants should be answered only through approved exercise mechanisms and in a manner consistent with the scenario timeline.

---

# Exercise Philosophy

Operation Red Horizon is designed to evaluate organizational decision-making rather than technical expertise.

The facilitator's role is **not** to teach, correct, or rescue participants during the exercise.

Instead, facilitators should:

* Encourage discussion.
* Clarify ambiguity when appropriate.
* Maintain scenario consistency.
* Keep the exercise aligned with learning objectives.
* Allow participants to experience the consequences of their decisions.

Learning emerges from reflection—not intervention.

---

# Exercise Control Structure

The exercise is managed through a coordinated Exercise Control Team.

```text id="4t8mzn"
Exercise Director
        │
Lead Facilitator
        │
────────────────────────────────────
│          │          │           │
White Cell Scribe  Observer    Technical
 Lead             Coordinator  Support
```

The Exercise Director retains overall authority.

The Lead Facilitator manages participant interaction.

The White Cell controls scenario progression.

Observers remain independent of exercise play.

---

# Roles and Responsibilities

## Exercise Director

Responsible for:

* Strategic oversight.
* Approval of significant exercise changes.
* Managing executive observers.
* Resolving governance issues.
* Authorizing PAUSE or STOP decisions.

---

## Lead Facilitator

Responsible for:

* Opening the exercise.
* Guiding discussion.
* Managing participant engagement.
* Maintaining timing.
* Coordinating with the White Cell.
* Conducting the Hot Wash.

The facilitator should avoid becoming the "expert in the room."

Instead, participants should explain their own decisions.

---

## White Cell Lead

Responsible for maintaining the integrity of the scenario.

Responsibilities include:

* Releasing injects.
* Answering requests.
* Managing scenario truth.
* Coordinating supporting facilitators.
* Tracking participant progress.
* Adjusting pacing when required.

The White Cell should speak with one consistent voice.

Contradictory answers reduce exercise realism.

---

## White Cell Analysts

Support the White Cell Lead by:

* Monitoring discussion.
* Preparing upcoming injects.
* Tracking assumptions.
* Managing artifact distribution.
* Recording unanswered questions.

Analysts should anticipate participant requests before they occur.

---

## Observer Coordinator

Coordinates all exercise observers.

Responsibilities include:

* Assigning observation areas.
* Consolidating observations.
* Ensuring consistent note-taking.
* Preparing the initial observation summary.

Observers document behaviour—they do not evaluate people.

---

## Scribe

Produces the official exercise record.

The record should include:

* Timeline.
* Decisions.
* Escalations.
* Assumptions.
* Key quotes.
* Requests for information.
* Outstanding issues.

The exercise timeline becomes an essential reference during the After Action Review.

---

# Learning Objectives

Facilitators should continually assess whether discussion is exploring the intended learning objectives.

Key objectives include:

* Cross-functional coordination.
* Executive decision-making.
* Third-party risk management.
* Incident escalation.
* Crisis communications.
* Business continuity.
* Recovery planning.
* Organizational governance.

The facilitator should gently redirect discussion if it becomes overly technical or diverges from these objectives.

---

# Scenario Truth

The following facts are considered true throughout the exercise.

Participants are **not** initially aware of these facts.

### Background

* A trusted maintenance vendor has experienced a credential compromise.
* The attacker has obtained valid VPN credentials.
* Initial access occurred several days before detection.
* The attacker has performed limited reconnaissance.
* No ransomware has yet been deployed.
* Sensitive information has already been staged for potential exfiltration.

These facts remain constant unless modified by the White Cell for exercise purposes.

---

# Hidden Assumptions

The following assumptions are known only to the exercise management team.

* Early alerts appear individually benign.
* Participants are unlikely to recognize the full attack chain immediately.
* Executive pressure increases over time.
* Media interest emerges only after operational disruption.
* Several injects intentionally introduce incomplete information.
* Contradictory reports are included to test information validation.

Participants should never be told these assumptions directly.

# Facilitation Principles

The facilitator should apply several guiding principles.

## Encourage Discussion

Use open-ended questions.

Examples include:

* "What concerns you most?"
* "Who else should be involved?"
* "What assumptions are you making?"
* "What evidence supports that conclusion?"
* "What could happen if we delay?"

---

## Resist Solving the Problem

Avoid statements such as:

* "You should..."
* "The correct answer is..."
* "Best practice says..."

Instead ask:

* "What options are available?"
* "What are the risks of each approach?"

---

## Balance Participation

Monitor participation carefully.

Invite quieter participants into the discussion.

Prevent dominant personalities from controlling decisions.

Ensure business representatives contribute alongside technical specialists.

---

## Maintain Realism

Participants should experience uncertainty.

Avoid answering questions that would not realistically have immediate answers.

If information is unavailable, say so.

---

# Exercise Pacing

The scenario is divided into several operational phases.

| Phase             | Facilitator Focus                        |
| ----------------- | ---------------------------------------- |
| Initial Detection | Observation and information gathering    |
| Investigation     | Hypothesis development                   |
| Escalation        | Governance and communication             |
| Containment       | Business versus technical trade-offs     |
| Crisis Management | Executive coordination                   |
| Recovery          | Business restoration and lessons learned |

Do not advance phases solely because time has elapsed.

Advance when meaningful discussion has occurred.

---

# Managing Information Requests

Participants will request additional information.

The White Cell should classify requests into one of four categories.

| Request Type                  | Response                                                         |
| ----------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Immediately Available         | Provide promptly.                                                |
| Available After Investigation | Delay until appropriate inject.                                  |
| Outside Scope                 | Explain that the information is unavailable within the exercise. |
| Unknown                       | Respond realistically: "That information is not yet available."  |

Avoid rewarding exhaustive questioning with unlimited information.

Real incidents rarely provide perfect visibility.

---

# Decision Adjudication

Participants may propose actions that significantly alter the scenario.

Examples include:

* Disabling all vendor access.
* Shutting down manufacturing.
* Disconnecting cloud services.
* Activating crisis management.
* Engaging law enforcement.

The White Cell should assess:

* Is the action reasonable?
* What would realistically happen next?
* Which injects require adjustment?
* What unintended consequences follow?

Scenario realism should always take precedence over preserving the original script.

---

# Contingency Planning

Exercises rarely unfold exactly as expected.

Common situations include:

### Participants progress faster than planned.

Response:

Accelerate inject delivery.

---

### Participants become stuck.

Response:

Introduce a clarifying inject or executive request.

---

### Discussion becomes overly technical.

Response:

Redirect toward business implications.

---

### Participants identify the entire scenario early.

Response:

Escalate the incident to test strategic decision-making rather than technical investigation.

---

### Time runs short.

Response:

Compress intermediate events while preserving key decision points.

---

# White Cell Communication

Exercise staff should maintain continuous communication using a dedicated coordination channel.

Suggested updates include:

* Inject delivered.
* Participant assumptions.
* Unexpected decisions.
* Timing adjustments.
* Observer concerns.
* Artifact readiness.

Consistent coordination prevents conflicting responses.

---

# Observation Priorities

Observers should pay particular attention to:

* Decision quality.
* Escalation timing.
* Communication effectiveness.
* Collaboration.
* Governance.
* Leadership.
* Risk management.
* Assumption validation.

These observations will later support the After Action Review.

---

# Facilitator Closing

The facilitator concludes the exercise by:

* Ending scenario play.
* Thanking participants.
* Conducting the Hot Wash.
* Reinforcing the no-blame philosophy.
* Explaining the After Action Review process.
* Outlining the improvement roadmap timeline.

Participants should leave understanding that the exercise is one step within a broader resilience improvement program.
