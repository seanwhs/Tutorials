# Appendix D — Sample Artifact Library

# Part 3 — Executive, Legal & Crisis Communications

> *A cybersecurity incident becomes a business crisis long before all the technical facts are known. Executives must make consequential decisions based on incomplete information while balancing operational continuity, legal obligations, customer trust, and organizational reputation.*

This section provides realistic communication artifacts that introduce executive pressure, legal considerations, regulatory obligations, and external stakeholder expectations into a tabletop exercise.

Unlike the technical alerts in earlier sections, these artifacts focus on **decision-making, governance, and communication** rather than investigation.

---

# Using Executive Communication Injects

These artifacts should generally be introduced **after participants have established that an incident may be credible**.

Typical trigger points include:

* Incident Response activation
* Executive notification
* Business disruption
* Data breach concerns
* Public service interruption
* Regulatory reporting requirements

The goal is not to overwhelm participants, but to test how effectively they coordinate across technical and business functions.

# Artifact 21 — CEO Status Request

## Exercise Objective

Test executive briefing discipline.

---

## Recommended Timing

T+45

---

## Delivery Method

* Email
* Chat
* Printed handout

---

## Facilitator Notes

Participants should decide:

* What information is sufficiently verified?
* What remains unknown?
* What recommendations should accompany the update?

Avoid allowing speculation.

---

### Sample Artifact

```text id="ceo-request"
Subject:
Status Update Requested

From:
Chief Executive Officer

Message

I understand there may be a cybersecurity issue affecting one of our vendors.

Please provide:

• Current situation
• Known business impact
• Immediate risks
• Recommended actions
• Next update time

I would like an update within 30 minutes.
```

# Artifact 24 — General Counsel Advice

## Exercise Objective

Introduce legal considerations.

---

```text id="legal-advice"
Legal Advisory

At this stage:

Do not speculate regarding attribution.

Preserve all investigation records.

Maintain chain of custody for evidence.

Do not notify external parties until facts have been verified.

Please coordinate all public communications through Legal and Corporate Communications.
```

# Artifact 25 — Regulatory Inquiry

## Exercise Objective

Test regulatory readiness.

---

```text id="regulator"
Regulatory Information Request

We are aware of media reports suggesting your organization may have experienced a cybersecurity incident.

Please advise whether:

• regulated systems are affected;
• customer information may be involved;
• your organization has initiated formal incident response.

Requested response within four hours.
```

# Artifact 26 — Media Inquiry

## Exercise Objective

# Optional Artifact — Press Holding Statement

Organizations often need a statement before the investigation concludes.

Example:

```text id="holding-statement"
We are investigating a technology issue affecting certain internal systems.

Our teams are actively assessing the situation.

At this time we have not confirmed the scope or cause of the incident.

We will provide additional information as our investigation progresses.
```

A holding statement should:

* acknowledge awareness,
* avoid speculation,
* demonstrate action,
* commit to future updates.

---

# Facilitator Guidance

These artifacts deliberately create executive pressure.

Participants should discuss:

* Who owns communications?
* What information is verified?
* What should remain confidential?
* Which stakeholders should be informed first?
* What approvals are required?

Encourage participants to distinguish between:

* facts,
* assumptions,
* hypotheses,
* recommendations.

# Cross-Functional Discussion Prompts

Use these questions to deepen discussion:

### Executive Leadership

* What decisions must be made immediately?
* What information is needed before escalating further?
* What business priorities must be protected?

### Legal

* Are notification obligations triggered?
* What evidence should be preserved?
* Which communications require legal review?

### Communications

* Should we issue a holding statement?
* How frequently should updates be provided?
* Who is authorized to speak publicly?

### Operations

* How will business continuity be maintained?
* Which services should receive priority?
* What customer commitments are at risk?

---

# Design Principles

Executive communication artifacts should create realistic organizational pressure without forcing predetermined decisions. They should encourage participants to balance transparency with accuracy, urgency with verification, and operational response with governance responsibilities.

Effective communication during a cybersecurity incident is not measured by how quickly information is released, but by whether stakeholders receive timely, accurate, and coordinated updates that support informed decision-making.
