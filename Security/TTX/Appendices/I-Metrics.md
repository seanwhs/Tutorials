# Appendix I — Cyber Resilience Metrics, KPIs, and Executive Reporting

## Enterprise Cyber Resilience Measurement Framework

> *"What cannot be measured cannot be systematically improved."*

---

# I.1 Purpose

This appendix defines a measurement framework for evaluating:

* Cyber resilience capability.
* Incident response maturity.
* Exercise effectiveness.
* Improvement progress.
* Executive readiness.

The objective is to move from:

```text
Exercise Completed
```

to:

```text
Capability Improved
```

---

# I.2 Measurement Principles

## Principle 1 — Measure Outcomes, Not Activity

Weak metric:

> Number of tabletop exercises completed.

Better metric:

> Percentage reduction in repeat findings after exercises.

---

## Principle 2 — Measure Improvement Over Time

A single measurement has limited value.

Organizations should track:

* Current state.
* Previous state.
* Target state.

---

## Principle 3 — Connect Cyber Metrics to Business Risk

Security metrics should explain:

* Operational impact.
* Financial risk.
* Customer impact.
* Business resilience.

---

## Principle 4 — Avoid Vanity Metrics

Examples of weak metrics:

* Number of alerts generated.
* Number of meetings held.
* Number of documents written.

These do not demonstrate resilience.

---

# I.3 Cyber Resilience Capability Model

The measurement framework evaluates six capability domains.

---

# Domain 1 — Detection Capability

Question:

> "Can we identify threats quickly?"

Measures:

* Detection speed.
* Visibility.
* Monitoring coverage.

---

# Domain 2 — Response Capability

Question:

> "Can we contain incidents effectively?"

Measures:

* Escalation speed.
* Incident coordination.
* Containment effectiveness.

---

# Domain 3 — Recovery Capability

Question:

> "Can we restore operations safely?"

Measures:

* Recovery confidence.
* Backup validation.
* Restoration success.

---

# Domain 4 — Governance Capability

Question:

> "Can leaders make effective decisions?"

Measures:

* Decision ownership.
* Risk acceptance.
* Crisis coordination.

---

# Domain 5 — Business Integration

Question:

> "Can cyber response support business priorities?"

Measures:

* Critical service mapping.
* Business participation.
* Continuity alignment.

---

# Domain 6 — Learning Capability

Question:

> "Does the organization improve after incidents?"

Measures:

* Finding closure.
* Repeat issue reduction.
* Exercise maturity.

---

# I.5 Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

---

# KPI-001 — Mean Time to Detect (MTTD)

## Purpose

Measures how quickly threats are identified.

---

## Formula

```
Time Incident Occurs
-
Time Incident Detected
```

---

## Improvement Goal

Reduce detection delay.

---

## Example Target

Reduce average detection time by 30%.

---

# KPI-002 — Mean Time to Respond (MTTR)

## Purpose

Measures response speed.

---

## Formula

```
Time Containment Begins
-
Time Incident Confirmed
```

---

## Improvement Goal

Increase containment speed.

---

# KPI-003 — Escalation Effectiveness

Measures:

* Correct escalation.
* Timeliness.
* Ownership clarity.

---

Example:

| Metric                          | Result |
| ------------------------------- | ------ |
| Correct severity classification | 95%    |
| Escalation within SLA           | 90%    |

---

# KPI-004 — Recovery Readiness

Measures:

* Successful restore tests.
* Recovery time achievement.
* Validation completion.

---

Example:

| Metric                          | Target    |
| ------------------------------- | --------- |
| Critical systems restore tested | 100%      |
| Recovery procedures validated   | Quarterly |

---

# KPI-005 — Third-Party Resilience

Measures:

* Vendor assessment completion.
* Privileged access reviews.
* Supplier incident readiness.

---

Example:

| Metric                    | Target    |
| ------------------------- | --------- |
| Critical vendors assessed | 100%      |
| Vendor access reviewed    | Quarterly |

---

# KPI-006 — Exercise Improvement Rate

Measures whether exercises create improvement.

---

Formula:

```
Closed Exercise Findings
-----------------------
Total Exercise Findings
```

---

Example:

90% closure rate.

---

# I.6 Key Risk Indicators (KRIs)

KPIs measure performance.

KRIs measure exposure.

---

# KRI-001 — Critical Unpatched Systems

Question:

How much exposure exists?

---

# KRI-002 — Excessive Privileged Access

Question:

How much identity risk exists?

---

# KRI-003 — Unsupported Technology

Question:

Where does resilience depend on outdated systems?

---

# KRI-004 — Recovery Dependency Risk

Question:

Can critical operations recover?

---

# KRI-005 — Third-Party Concentration Risk

Question:

Could supplier failure disrupt operations?

---

# I.10 Board-Level Reporting Principles

Board reporting should focus on:

## Business Impact

Not:

> "We blocked 2 million attacks."

> "Critical services can recover within defined business tolerance."

* What happened.
* Why it matters.
* What decision is required.

# I.12 Common Measurement Mistakes

---

## Mistake 1 — Measuring Security Activity

Example:

"Number of alerts."

Problem:

Does not show resilience.

---

## Mistake 2 — Ignoring Business Outcomes

Example:

"Firewall blocked attacks."

Question:

Could the business continue operating?

---

## Mistake 3 — Not Tracking Improvement

Finding without closure tracking creates repeated failures.
