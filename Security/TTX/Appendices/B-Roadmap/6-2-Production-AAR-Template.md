# Closing Statement

The purpose of an After Action Review is not to document what went wrong—it is to improve how the organization prepares for future incidents.

A successful AAR transforms observations into evidence, evidence into findings, findings into recommendations, and recommendations into measurable improvements. When integrated into governance processes and validated through future exercises, the AAR becomes one of the most valuable instruments for strengthening organizational cyber resilience.

This report should be treated as a living document. Progress should be reviewed regularly, improvement actions should be tracked to completion, and future tabletop exercises should validate that corrective actions have achieved their intended outcomes.
