# Appendix B — Templates Library

# Part 7.1 — Participant Invitation & Exercise Briefing Package

> *Well-prepared participants spend less time understanding the exercise and more time practicing effective incident response.*

The participant briefing package establishes expectations before the exercise begins. It ensures every participant understands the purpose of the exercise, their role, the rules of engagement, and the logistics for the session.

A well-designed briefing package reduces confusion, minimizes administrative interruptions, and allows facilitators to focus on meaningful discussion rather than repeatedly explaining exercise mechanics.

This package should normally be distributed **five to ten business days** before the exercise.

---

# Package Contents

A complete participant package should include:

* Participant Invitation
* Executive Summary
* Exercise Logistics
* Rules of Engagement
* Scenario Overview
* Participant Responsibilities
* Preparation Checklist
* Frequently Asked Questions
* Day-of Reminder

Not every exercise requires every section, but consistency improves participant readiness.

# Template 5 — Participant Responsibilities

Participants should:

* engage actively,
* collaborate respectfully,
* explain their reasoning,
* ask questions,
* challenge assumptions professionally,
* document decisions,
* remain within exercise scope.

Participants should not:

* attempt technical investigations outside the exercise,
* access production systems,
* contact external organizations,
* perform real incident response activities.

# Template 7 — Exercise Etiquette

Participants are encouraged to:

* Listen carefully.
* Allow others to contribute.
* Ask clarifying questions.
* Respect differing opinions.
* Focus on organizational learning.

Avoid:

* assigning blame,
* dominating discussion,
* debating unrelated issues,
* pursuing technical implementation details beyond the exercise objectives.

---

# Template 8 — Frequently Asked Questions

## Is this a test?

No.

The exercise evaluates organizational processes rather than individual performance.

---

## Can we make mistakes?

Yes.

Exercises are designed to expose gaps in a safe learning environment.

---

## Should we use our real procedures?

Yes.

Respond as you would during an actual incident.

---

## Can we ask questions?

Absolutely.

Facilitators are available to clarify exercise information.

---

## What happens after the exercise?

Observers and facilitators prepare an After Action Review.

Improvement recommendations are assigned to organizational owners.

# Participant Briefing Principles

A successful tabletop exercise begins long before participants enter the room.

Participants who understand the objectives, logistics, and expectations arrive prepared to collaborate rather than orient themselves. The briefing package establishes psychological safety, reinforces the no-blame philosophy, and ensures that everyone starts with a shared understanding of the exercise framework.

The goal is not to teach participants how to solve the scenario. It is to prepare them to engage confidently, communicate effectively, and contribute to a realistic, collaborative discussion.
