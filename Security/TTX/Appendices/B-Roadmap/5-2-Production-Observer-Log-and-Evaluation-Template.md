# Observer Responsibilities

During the exercise you should:

* Observe silently.
* Record objective evidence.
* Capture timestamps whenever practical.
* Document participant assumptions.
* Record key decisions.
* Identify effective practices.
* Identify opportunities for improvement.
* Avoid coaching participants.
* Avoid solving the scenario.
* Preserve confidentiality.

# Appendix A — Observation Quality Checklist

Before submitting this workbook, verify that you have:

* Recorded timestamps for major events.
* Distinguished facts from opinions.
* Captured participant assumptions.
* Recorded significant decisions.
* Identified both strengths and improvement opportunities.
* Supported findings with evidence.
* Avoided assigning blame.
* Completed the capability assessment.
* Proposed actionable recommendations.
* Linked observations to potential AAR findings.

---

# Appendix B — Evidence Classification Guide

Use the following hierarchy when recording evidence.

| Evidence Type              | Reliability |
| -------------------------- | ----------- |
| Direct participant quote   | High        |
| Timestamped decision       | High        |
| Official exercise artifact | High        |
| Observer interpretation    | Medium      |
| Personal opinion           | Low         |

Whenever possible, support findings with multiple independent pieces of evidence.

---

# Appendix C — Observer Best Practices

Remember these guiding principles throughout the exercise:

* Observe more than you write.
* Listen before interpreting.
* Capture evidence before drawing conclusions.
* Document strengths as diligently as weaknesses.
* Focus on organizational capability rather than individual performance.
* Preserve context with timestamps and participant roles.
* Maintain confidentiality at all times.

A well-completed Observer Log is more than a notebook—it is the evidentiary foundation of the After Action Review. High-quality observations enable credible findings, meaningful recommendations, measurable improvement plans, and long-term organizational learning.
