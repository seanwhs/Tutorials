# Slide 61 – Risk-Based Exercise Planning

## Learning Objectives

* Understand why risk-based planning improves TTX effectiveness
* Align exercise scenarios with organizational priorities
* Focus resources on the highest-risk areas

### Key Points

* Every tabletop exercise should be driven by organizational risk.
* High-impact, high-likelihood threats should receive priority.
* Business objectives should guide scenario selection.
* Risk assessments provide evidence for exercise planning.

### Risk-Based Planning Process

1. Identify business objectives
2. Identify critical assets
3. Assess threats
4. Assess vulnerabilities
5. Evaluate business impact
6. Prioritize scenarios
7. Define exercise scope

### Speaker Notes

Organizations have limited resources and cannot exercise every possible cyber scenario. Risk-based planning ensures that tabletop exercises focus on the incidents most likely to disrupt critical operations or cause significant financial, legal, or reputational damage. By aligning exercises with enterprise risk assessments, organizations maximize the value of their investment.

# Slide 76 – Introduction to Cyber Threat Intelligence (CTI)

## Learning Objectives

* Define Cyber Threat Intelligence (CTI)
* Understand the value of intelligence-driven tabletop exercises
* Differentiate intelligence from raw data

### What is Cyber Threat Intelligence?

Cyber Threat Intelligence (CTI) is evidence-based information about cyber threats, threat actors, motivations, capabilities, tactics, techniques, and procedures (TTPs) that enables informed security decisions.

### Why CTI Matters

* Improves situational awareness
* Enables proactive defense
* Prioritizes security investments
* Supports realistic tabletop scenarios
* Enhances incident response planning
* Informs executive decision-making

### CTI vs. Data

| Data     | Information        | Intelligence       |
| -------- | ------------------ | ------------------ |
| Raw logs | Organized findings | Actionable insight |

### Speaker Notes

Cyber Threat Intelligence transforms technical indicators, threat reports, and observed adversary behaviors into actionable knowledge. Instead of designing tabletop exercises around generic attacks, organizations should use current intelligence to simulate realistic scenarios based on actual adversaries targeting their industry.

# Slide 82 – Techniques and Procedures

## Learning Objectives

* Differentiate tactics, techniques, and procedures
* Understand TTPs

### Definitions

**Tactic**
The adversary's objective.

**Technique**
How the objective is achieved.

**Procedure**
A specific implementation of a technique.

### Example

Tactic:
Credential Access

Technique:
Credential Dumping

Procedure:
Using Mimikatz against Active Directory

### Why TTPs Matter

* Repeatable adversary behaviors
* Better scenario realism
* Improved detection planning

### Speaker Notes

While malware changes rapidly, adversary behaviors often remain consistent. Designing exercises around TTPs rather than specific malware families makes scenarios more durable and broadly applicable.

```text
Business Objectives
        ↓
Critical Assets
        ↓
Threat Actors
        ↓
Attack Vectors
        ↓
Business Impact
        ↓
Exercise Scenario
```
