# Cybersecurity Threat Modeling 

This directory contains an instructional deck for conducting professional Cybersecurity Threat Modeling exercises.

## Overview

Threat modeling is a structured, repeatable process for identifying, quantifying, and addressing security threats. This material is designed for security architects, developers, and risk managers to shift security "left" by identifying vulnerabilities during the design phase rather than in production.

## Content Summary

The slides are organized into the following logical sections:

### Foundations & Strategy 

* **Business Value:** Explaining why design-phase security reduces costs and meets regulatory requirements (NIST SSDF, PCI DSS 4.0, GDPR).


* **Core Principles:** Establishing the bedrock of resilient architecture including Least Privilege, Defense in Depth, and Zero Trust.



### Frameworks & Methodologies 

* **STRIDE:** Deep-dive analysis into Spoofing, Tampering, Repudiation, Information Disclosure, Denial of Service, and Elevation of Privilege.


* **Risk Scoring:** Usage of DREAD and CVSS v3.1 for quantitative and qualitative prioritization.


* **Process Models:** Detailed coverage of PASTA, VAST, LINDDUN, and Trike.


* **DFDs:** Visualizing systems via Context, Level 1, and Level 2 Data Flow Diagrams.



### Advanced & Modern Contexts 

* **Technical Domains:** Specialized threat modeling for Cloud-native, Kubernetes, API, and AI/LLM architectures.


* **Zero Trust:** Aligning STRIDE methodology with Zero Trust Architecture (ZTA) and continuous verification.


* **DevSecOps:** Integrating threat modeling into CI/CD pipelines to prevent "threat drift".



### Governance & Operations 

* **Compliance:** NIST SSDF (SP 800-218) integration and audit evidence collection.


* **Governance:** Architecture Review Boards (ARB) and Architecture Decision Records (ADR).


* **Case Studies:** A full retail banking platform end-to-end threat model analysis.


* **Tooling:** An overview of the ecosystem including Microsoft Threat Modeling Tool, OWASP Threat Dragon, and IriusRisk.



## Usage Guidelines

* **Minimalist Design:** The slides follow a strict white-background, black-text design for maximum readability and printing flexibility.


* **Continuous Improvement:** These slides are designed to be "living artifacts"—they should be updated quarterly or upon any significant architecture change.


* **Customization:** Use the provided templates (Project Charter, Threat Register, Executive Report) to tailor the deliverables to your specific organizational risk appetite.
