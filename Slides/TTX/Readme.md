This directory contains the core curriculum and supporting resources for the **"The Cybersecurity Tabletop Masterclass."**

The materials are organized into the following logical components:

### 1. Program Foundations & Methodology

* **Exercise Lifecycle:** Detailed phases from planning and threat intelligence to after-action reporting and continuous improvement.


* **Governance Framework:** Guidelines for establishing executive sponsorship, steering committees, and clear Rules of Engagement (RoE).


* **Maturity Model:** A four-level progression framework for incident readiness, ranging from discussion-based exercises (L1/L2) to full-scale operations (L3/L4).



### 2. Scenario Design & Threat Modeling

* **Design Principles:** Instructions for creating risk-driven, business-focused scenarios that prioritize decision-making over dramatic suspense.


* **Threat Intelligence Integration:** Guidance on mapping adversary TTPs (using MITRE ATT&CK) to specific organizational assets and business processes.


* **Master Scenario Events List (MSEL):** Templates and techniques for sequencing injects as a "decision clock" to manage exercise pace and complexity.



### 3. Execution & Facilitation

* **Facilitator Toolkit:** Techniques for "nudge" questioning, managing group dynamics, and maintaining psychological safety without acting as an "answer key".


* **White Cell Operations:** Procedures for managing the control room, pacing the crisis, and ensuring consistency across all inject deliveries.


* **Inject Management:** A comprehensive library of inject types (technical, executive, legal, HR, etc.) and guidance on branching logic to adapt to participant responses.



### 4. Evaluation & Improvement

* **Observation & Scoring:** Frameworks for objective, evidence-based assessment of decision quality, communication, and team collaboration.


* **After Action Reporting (AAR):** Templates for documenting findings, conducting root cause analysis (RCA), and prioritizing corrective actions.


* **Sustainability:** Strategies for tracking corrective actions and aligning tabletop exercises with broader Enterprise Risk Management (ERM) and regulatory requirements.



### 5. Supplemental Templates & Tools

This collection includes the necessary administrative and technical artifacts to support an exercise program:

* **Administrative:** Exercise Charter, Planning Checklists, and Participant Guides.
* **Operational:** Inject Tracking Sheets, Decision Logs, and Observer Evaluation Forms.
* **Reporting:** AAR Templates and Corrective Action Registers.

---

> **Facilitator's Rule:** Never run L3 or L4 exercises until your L1/L2 findings are fully closed and owned. The primary goal of any tabletop exercise is to expose the critical gap between documented process and lived behavior in a safe, bounded environment.
